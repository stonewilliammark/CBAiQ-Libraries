## Solution overview

### What this solution is

Market Monitor iQ - Brand is a recurring subscription that benchmarks a client's brand against a defined market and competitor set. It produces a monthly view of market share, performance drivers, customer composition and loyalty, with adjacent industry context to help interpret what the brand's numbers mean. It is delivered through the Market Monitor iQ web portal, with an Excel data extract for the full dataset.

### What problem or need it addresses

Market Monitor iQ - Brand addresses a common blind spot for consumer-facing brands: they may know a lot about what is happening within their own business, but have less visibility of how they are performing relative to the wider market, which customer groups they are winning or losing, and where competitors are capturing spend. The solution helps contextualise performance, track market share, understand growth drivers, and see where customers also spend.

### Who it is for

Retail and consumer-facing brands that want an ongoing, externally benchmarked view of their performance — typically those large enough to define a meaningful competitor set and a reporting industry context. It tends to be bought by brand, category, insights or strategy teams who need a recurring read on relative performance rather than a one-off study.

### Positioning

Position this as a recurring brand performance monitoring and benchmarking solution. The value is in showing how a brand is tracking relative to market, competitors and customer wallet, rather than reporting internal brand trends. Lead with relative performance, market share, customer mix and loyalty, and bring the driver tree in to explain what is moving the numbers.

### What this solution is for

- Tracking brand performance against a defined market and competitor set
- Understanding market share and the drivers behind it
- Identifying which customer groups are over or under-performing
- Understanding customer loyalty and competitor brand preferences
- Providing adjacent industry context to inform strategy

### What this solution is not for

- Raw customer-level data access
- Direct activation or campaign execution
- Detailed causal diagnosis on its own without additional client context

## Customer needs and decisions supported


### Customer needs addressed

Clients buy Market Monitor iQ - Brand when they need an ongoing, market-contextualised read on how their brand is performing. The underlying needs are to know whether they are outperforming or lagging the market, what is driving growth or decline, which customer groups they are winning or losing, and where competitors are capturing wallet. It exists because internal brand reporting alone does not answer these questions — they require a normalised, external benchmark.

### Common questions

The questions clients ask in this space cluster around the following core themes.

**Core question 1 — How is my brand performing relative to market?**

- How is my market share trending and where am I gaining or losing it?
- How does my performance compare with my chosen competitor set?
- How does this look across in-store, online or both?
- How does it look at national versus state level?

**Core question 2 — What is driving my performance?**

- Is movement coming from customer penetration, frequency, spend per customer or spend per transaction?
- Where is the driver tree pointing — acquisition, loyalty or basket size?

**Core question 3 — Which customer groups are driving or holding back performance?**

- How are different age, affluence and life stage groups performing?
- How does this differ between metro and regional?
- Where is there headroom in specific demographic groups?

**Core question 4 — How loyal are my customers and where else do they shop?**

- What share of wallet do I hold among my customers?
- Which competitor brands do my customers also transact with?
- How does my brand penetration and affinity compare to a benchmark?

### Supporting decisions


| Decision | How this solution helps | What it does not answer on its own |
|---|---|---|
| How are we performing versus market? | Provides market share, market sales context, and brand performance measures versus market and competitor groups. | It does not explain all internal business causes without other context. |
| Where is our growth or decline coming from? | Shows driver tree metrics including customer penetration, spend per customer, spend per transaction, and frequency. | It does not isolate the effect of pricing, ranging, media, or store execution on its own. |
| Which customer groups should we focus on? | Shows performance across age, affluence, life stage, and metro versus regional groupings. | It does not provide campaign or proposition design by itself. |
| How loyal are our customers and who else do they shop with? | Shows share of wallet, wallet trends, and competitor brand preferences. | It does not support direct customer identification or activation. |
| Where are there growth opportunities by geography or channel? | Provides national and state views, plus in-store, online, or combined channel views. | It does not replace detailed location planning or site feasibility work. |

### How the data answers these questions


| Question | Data and capability used | How a client lead would walk through it |
|---|---|---|
| How is my brand performing relative to market? | Brand Share, Market/Industry Sales, driver metrics at Brand, Market and Industry levels; Channel and State dimensions | Start with Brand Share trend at the chosen aggregation level. Compare with Market/Industry Sales trend to show whether share movement reflects share gain or category growth. Cut by Channel and State to localise the story. |
| What is driving my performance? | Customer penetration, Spend per Customer, Spend per transaction, Transaction frequency at Brand level | Walk down the driver tree from total spend to customer penetration and the per-customer drivers. Identify which lever is moving against market and against the same period a year ago. |
| Which customer groups are driving or holding back performance? | Demographics — Sales Contribution, Demographics — Market share, Demographics — Sales by Age, Affluence and Lifestage; Metro/Regional split (when delivered) | Look at Sales Contribution to size each group, then Market share by demographic to show where the brand is over or under-performing. Layer in metro versus regional where relevant. |
| How loyal are my customers and where else do they shop? | Share of wallet, Brand penetration, Brand affinity, Spend with Competitors at L12M; Competitor brand dimension | Use share of wallet and L12M brand penetration to size loyalty. Use brand affinity and spend with competitors to identify which competitor brands are capturing the rest of the customer's wallet. |

### Common customer questions or objections

**Q: Can the market and competitor set be customised?**
Yes. Bespoke market definitions and competitor groups are supported, subject to minimum composition rules captured in the engagement letter and bank compliance checks.

**Q: Can results be broken down by channel and geography?**
Yes. Channel cuts cover in-store, online or both. Geography is available at national and state level today; metro versus regional and bespoke regions (e.g. SA4, TV regions) are scoped but not yet live.

**Q: Will all requested outputs always be available?**
No. Reporting is subject to minimum sample size thresholds and compliance rules, and some results may be aggregated or omitted where thresholds are not met.


## Delivery options and capabilities

### Delivery options

Market Monitor iQ (Brand) is delivered as a monthly subscription with two channels: the Market Monitor iQ web portal and an Excel data extract. The portal is the lighter dashboard view; the Excel is the full data extract. The portal does not currently carry YoY monthly views, market share contribution by month, or sales contribution by month — clients who need those views rely on the Excel. The standard subscription term is 12 months, with monthly data refresh and up to 48 months of historical data.

### Core capabilities

| Capability area | Capability |
|---|---|
| Market share | Brand Share against a defined market and competitor set, with bespoke market definitions supported. |
| Performance drivers | Driver tree covering customer penetration, spend per customer, spend per transaction and transaction frequency, at Brand, Market and Industry levels. |
| Customers | Sales contribution, market share and sales views across age, affluence and life stage demographics. |
| Loyalty | Share of wallet, share of wallet change, brand penetration, brand affinity and spend with competitors at rolling 12-month level. |
| Industry context | Adjacent industry sales and driver metrics to contextualise brand performance. |

### Edge cases and exceptions

The components above describe the standard solution as it is delivered today. The following items are exceptions worth being aware of when discussing scope with clients. Items captured in the Excel but not currently delivered are listed separately in the appendix parking lot.

**Constraints on what's available.**

- Share of wallet, spend with competitors and brand penetration are available at L12M / annual rolling level only, and at national level only. Brand affinity is also available at L12M total only; the Excel does not record an explicit geographic constraint against this metric.
- Competitor brand dimension is available for Brand Penetration, Brand Affinity and Competitor Influence metrics only, and at annual level only.
- The portal delivery channel does not carry YoY monthly views, market share contribution by month, or sales contribution by month — these are available in the Excel extract.
- Demographics — Sales is available in the UI but not in the Excel extract.
- Spend with Competitors is available in the UI; in the Excel extract it is inferred rather than explicitly delivered.

**Compliance and operational.**

- All outputs are subject to minimum sample size thresholds and bank compliance rules; some results may be aggregated or omitted.
- Bespoke competitor markets must pass bank compliance and sample checks before being accepted into a client configuration.

## Relationship to other solutions

### Relationship to adjacent solutions

Market Monitor iQ - Brand sits within the broader Market Monitor iQ family alongside Market Monitor iQ - Industries and Market Monitor iQ - Venue. They share common data foundations, recurring monitoring logic and similar driver metrics, but Brand is centred on a focal brand and its relative position in market, where Industries is centred on category-level performance and Venue on location-level performance.

Within Consumer Brands, Market Monitor - Brand sits alongside Profile iQ (deep customer profiling) and Enrich iQ (customer base enrichment). These are typically sold to the same client teams but answer different questions.

### Known overlaps

There is overlap with Market Monitor iQ - Industries on industry trend monitoring, performance metrics, demographic cuts, and geography and channel views. There is overlap with Profile iQ on demographic-level customer views — though the depth of customer drill-down differs materially.

The practical distinction:

- Market Monitor iQ - Brand answers, "How is my brand performing relative to market and competitors?"
- Market Monitor iQ - Industries answers, "How are categories, industries or channels performing at a market level?"
- Profile iQ answers, "Who are my customers and what do they look like in detail?"

## Risks, gaps and dependencies

### Known gaps or ambiguities

Current materials are not fully consistent on delivery language, with references to both portal and Excel delivery without always making clear which channel carries which views. The portal and Excel each cover a different cut of the solution rather than one being a subset of the other — the portal lacks some month-level views (YoY monthly, market share contribution by month, sales contribution by month), while the Excel lacks some metrics that are present in the portal (Demographics — Sales, Spend with Competitors). The family has also evolved over time, so packaging and wording is not yet fully standardised.

### Key risks in how the solution is described or applied

- Describing the solution too broadly and blurring it with the Industries variant, since the underlying metrics are similar but the unit of analysis is different. 
- Overstating precision — sample size thresholds, compliance rules, and aggregation requirements mean not every requested cut will be available, and clients sometimes expect more granularity than the solution can deliver. 
- Loyalty being mis-sold: brand-specific loyalty metrics are only available at national, L12M level, and clients sometimes expect them at finer granularity.

### Dependencies

- Agreed brand, market, industry and competitor definitions before delivery
- Minimum sample sizes and compliance approval for output visibility
- Client onboarding and authorised user list
- Ongoing monthly data update cycle
