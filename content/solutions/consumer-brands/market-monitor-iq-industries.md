## Solution overview

### What this solution is

Market Monitor iQ - Industries is a recurring subscription that monitors industry and category performance using a weighted, externally normalised market view. It produces industry-level sales, growth drivers, demographic mix, and channel and geography cuts on a recurring (typically monthly) basis, with the ability to compare across multiple industries and drill into a single industry. It is delivered monthly through a web-based access portal, with an Excel-based dashboard or data output available for download.

### What problem or need it addresses

Market Monitor iQ - Industries addresses the need for a robust market view where the core question is about category, channel or industry performance rather than direct brand benchmarking. It helps clients understand which markets are growing or declining, what is driving that movement, and where there are opportunities by segment, channel or geography.

### Who it is for

This solution suits clients who need an industry or category view rather than a direct brand benchmark. Typical clients include FMCG manufacturers, media businesses, and PE or investment players that want to understand which markets are winning or losing and how channels are performing. Current active clients sit across FMCG, hospitality groups, telecommunications and media, and investment research.

### Positioning

Position this as an industry performance monitoring solution. Lead with market movement, category trends, growth drivers, demographic mix, and channel or geographic variation. Frame it as a way to understand where to play, what is changing, and which parts of the market are driving performance.

### What this solution is for

- Tracking industry sales and growth trends over time
- Understanding what is driving category growth or decline through driver metrics
- Comparing performance across industries
- Understanding the demographic composition of category spend and how it is changing
- Tracking differences by state, channel, and industry definition

### What this solution is not for

- Direct brand market share benchmarking against named competitors
- Named competitor brand comparison
- Loyalty and share of wallet analysis for a focal brand

## Customer needs and decisions supported

### Customer needs addressed

Clients buy this solution when they need to understand category-level performance and what is shaping it. The need typically combines four things at once: which industries or channels are growing fastest, what is driving category movement, which customer groups are shaping that performance, and how performance varies by geography and channel. The data informs investment, planning, distribution, and market prioritisation decisions.

### Common questions

The questions clients ask in this space cluster around four core themes.

**Core question 1 — How is my industry or category performing?**

- Which industries or channels are growing fastest?
- How is category performance changing over time?
- How does performance differ by state or channel?

**Core question 2 — What is driving category growth or decline?**

- Are more customers entering the category, or are existing customers spending more?
- Is transaction frequency or transaction size changing?
- Where is the movement coming from in the driver metrics?

**Core question 3 — Which customer groups are shaping category performance?**

- How is category spend distributed across age, affluence, and life stage?
- How is the demographic mix changing over time?

**Core question 4 — Where should we prioritise by geography or channel?**

- Which states are driving category growth?
- How is the online versus in-store split moving?

### Supporting decisions

Where the questions above tie to specific decisions, those are noted below:

- Industry and channel prioritisation — informs decisions on where to focus market investment, planning and distribution emphasis; does not define a full go-to-market strategy on its own.
- Category growth or decline — informs whether movement appears linked to penetration, frequency, transaction size or spend per customer; does not prove causality without external context.
- Segment focus — informs which demographic groups are shaping category performance and where product or marketing investment may need to shift; does not provide customer-level activation or targeting lists.
- Geography and channel focus — informs where growth or gaps are concentrated across state and online/in-store views; does not replace detailed local market modelling.
- External market benchmarking — supports comparison of internal results against an external market view; does not reconcile internal sales data by default.

### How the data answers these questions

| Question | Data and capability used | How a client lead would walk through it |
|---|---|---|
| Which industries or channels are growing fastest? | Market/Industry Sales metric across multiple industries, by Channel and State, at weekly, monthly and yearly views including YoY. | Compare Sales and Sales growth across industries on the monthly and yearly views. Cut by Channel and State to see where the movement is concentrated. |
| What is driving growth or decline in a category? | Customer penetration, Spend per customer, Spend per transaction, Transaction frequency, by Industry at monthly and yearly views including YoY. | Within a single industry, walk down the driver tree from total spend to customer penetration and the per-customer drivers. Identify which lever is moving against the same period a year ago. |
| Which customer groups are shaping category performance? | Customer-category metrics — Demographics — Sales Contribution, Sales Growth, and Sales — cut by Age, Affluence, or Lifestage. | Open the demographic view for the industry of interest. Use Sales Contribution to size each group, then Sales Growth to show where the movement is, then Sales for the underlying scale. |
| Where should we prioritise by geography or channel? | Driver metrics across State (including National) and Channel (Online, In-Store, Total). | Compare industry performance across states and across channels to identify where the strongest growth or biggest gaps sit. |

### Common customer questions or objections

**Q: Can industries be standard or bespoke?**
Both. Standard Market Monitor iQ industry groupings are pre-approved for compliance and faster to onboard. Bespoke industry groupings can be defined client by client but carry higher onboarding cost.

**Q: How current is the data?**
Monthly data is typically available by the end of the third week of the following period.

**Q: Are the outputs weighted and representative?**
Yes. Outputs are weighted to reflect actual Australian spending behaviour, with Census weighting applied.

**Q: Can this be broken down by channel or geography?**
Yes. Outputs can be cut by online, in-store, or total, and by national or state view. Metro versus regional and bespoke regions (e.g. SA4, TV regions) are scoped but not yet live.

## Delivery options and capabilities

### Delivery options

Market Monitor iQ - Industries is delivered monthly through a web-based access portal, with an Excel-based dashboard or data output available for download from the portal. The standard subscription term is 12 months, with monthly updates and 48 months of historical data up to the month preceding the contract start date.

### Core capabilities

| Capability area | Capability |
|---|---|
| Industry sales and growth | Tracks industry-level sales over time, with weekly, monthly, and yearly views including YoY change, to monitor changes and key events. |
| Driver analysis | Decomposes industry performance into customer penetration, spend per customer, spend per transaction and transaction frequency. |
| Demographic breakdowns | Shows how industry sales contribution, growth, and totals are distributed across age, affluence and life stage segments. |
| Industry comparison and detail | Lets users compare multiple industries side by side and drill into a single industry by demographic, channel or geography. |

- 48 months of historical data
- Monthly and rolling 12-month views
- Standard and bespoke industry definitions

### Edge cases and exceptions

The components above describe the standard solution as it is delivered today. The following items are exceptions worth being aware of when discussing scope with clients. Items captured in the Excel but not currently delivered are listed separately in the appendix parking lot.

**Constraints on what's available.**

- Market/Industry Sales is the only Driver metric available at weekly granularity; the other Driver metrics (Customer penetration, Spend per customer, Spend per transaction, Transaction frequency) are available at monthly and yearly only, plus YoY.
- The Customer metrics (Demographics — Sales Contribution, Sales Growth, Sales) are cut by Age, Affluence, or Lifestage only — Gender is not currently a live demographic dimension.
- Geographic cuts available today are state-level only (NSW, VIC, QLD, SA, WA, TAS, NT, ACT, National). Metro versus regional and bespoke regions are not currently live.
- Quarterly time views are not currently live.

**Compliance and operational.**

- All outputs are subject to minimum sample size thresholds and bank compliance rules; some results may be aggregated or omitted.
- Bespoke industry groupings must pass bank compliance and sample checks before being accepted into a client configuration; standard Market Monitor iQ industry groupings are pre-approved.

## Relationship to other solutions

### Relationship to adjacent solutions

Market Monitor iQ - Industries sits within the broader Market Monitor iQ family alongside Market Monitor iQ - Brand and Market Monitor iQ (Venue). They share common data foundations, recurring monitoring logic and similar driver metrics, but Industries is centred on category-level performance with no focal brand at the centre, where Brand is centred on a focal brand and its relative position in market, and Venue on location-level performance.

Within Consumer Brands, Market Monitor iQ - Industries sits alongside Profile iQ (deep customer profiling) and Enrich iQ (customer base enrichment). These are typically sold to the same client teams but answer different questions.

### Known overlaps

There is overlap with Market Monitor iQ - Brand on performance metrics, demographic cuts, geography and channel views, and the recurring market monitoring structure. There is overlap with Profile iQ on demographic-level customer views — though the depth of customer drill-down differs materially.

The practical distinction:

- Market Monitor iQ - Industries answers, "How is my industry or category performing, and what is driving that?"
- Market Monitor iQ - Brand answers, "How is my focal brand performing relative to market and named competitors?"
- Profile iQ answers, "Who are my customers and what do they look like in detail?"

## Risks, gaps and dependencies

### Known gaps or ambiguities

Some family materials use similar language across Market Monitor iQ Brand and Industries, which can make the distinction between the two less crisp than it should be. There is also variance in how the wider family is packaged and described across documents. The family has evolved over time, so packaging and wording is not yet fully standardised.

### Key risks in how the solution is described or applied

- Selling this as though it provides focal brand benchmarking when it is designed for industry and category monitoring — Industries is not the right fit for a client whose primary need is named competitor comparison. 
- Underselling it as simple descriptive reporting, when current materials clearly support growth driver analysis and segment-level interpretation.
- Overstating precision: sample size thresholds, compliance rules, and aggregation requirements mean not every requested cut will be available.

### Dependencies

- Agreement on standard or bespoke industry definitions before delivery
- Minimum sample sizes and compliance approval for output visibility
- Client onboarding and authorised user list
- Ongoing monthly data update cycle
