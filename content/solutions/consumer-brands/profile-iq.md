## Solution overview

### What this solution is

Profile iQ is a customer profiling solution that helps brands understand who their customers are beyond their spend with the brand. It produces aggregated insights across a defined customer cohort, including demographics, category and brand behaviours, online share of spend, and where those customers are located. It is typically delivered as a one-off Excel dashboard, often accompanied by a short PDF summary insights pack.

### What problem or need it addresses

Many brands know who buys from them at a basic level, but not how those customers behave more broadly across categories, brands, channels and life stages. That limits segmentation, targeting, acquisition strategy, retention strategy, partnership planning and broader commercial decision-making. Profile iQ closes that gap by giving clients a fuller view of who their customers are and how they behave outside the brand.

### Who it is for

Profile iQ is primarily suited to consumer brands, particularly mid-market to enterprise clients. The main buyer and user personas are marketing leaders and brand managers, though the outputs can also support broader commercial and strategy conversations. It is relevant where a client wants to understand their total brand customers, segments such as heavy/medium/light or new/lapsed/continuing, geography or demographic-based cohorts, or bespoke and threshold-based cohorts.

### Positioning

Position Profile iQ as a customer intelligence solution grounded in real spend behaviour, not survey responses or claimed preferences. It helps clients understand who their customers are, where else they spend, which categories and brands matter to them, and where there may be growth, competitor or partnership opportunities. Lead with deeper customer understanding, sharper segmentation and growth choices, partnership and adjacency opportunities, and competitor and market context.

### What this solution is for

- Understanding the make-up of a current or target customer cohort
- Identifying meaningful customer segments
- Informing acquisition and retention strategy
- Identifying cross-shop patterns, competitors and partnership opportunities
- Informing strategic marketing, range, media and location decisions

### What this solution is not for

- Individual-level targeting or activation
- Sharing personal information
- Replacing campaign execution tools
- Answering every execution question on its own

A practical way to frame the boundary is that Profile iQ helps define and understand the customer opportunity, while downstream solutions such as Enrich iQ support execution.

## Customer needs and decisions supported

### Customer needs addressed

Profile iQ addresses needs around understanding who the current customer really is, identifying under-reached or higher-potential customer groups, improving acquisition and retention strategy, finding relevant partners, media channels and adjacencies, understanding where customers are spending outside the brand, validating or challenging internal assumptions with behavioural data, and informing product, range, location and strategic planning.

### Common questions

The questions clients ask in this space cluster around the following core themes. Sub-questions underneath each core question are the more specific asks that come up in client conversations.

**Core question 1 — Which customer segments matter most for our growth?**

- How does my customer base split by demographics, affluence and lifestage?
- Where does my cohort over- or under-index against the broader market?
- Where might under-reached or higher-potential segments sit?

**Core question 2 — How should we shape our acquisition and retention strategy?**

- What do my target customers look like across categories and brands?
- Where else do they spend their money?
- Which channels and behaviours are most relevant to them?

**Core question 3 — Who are our true competitors and what else is winning share with our customers?**

- Which other brands do my customers transact with?
- Which adjacent categories matter most to them?
- Are there indirect or emerging competitors we should be aware of?

**Core question 4 — Which partnerships, geographies or adjacencies are most relevant?**

- Where do my customers disproportionately spend that could inform partnership opportunities?
- Where are my customers concentrated geographically?
- Which categories or occasions could the brand expand into?

### Supporting decisions

Where the questions above tie to specific decisions, those are noted below:

- Which customer segments to prioritise — informs segmentation strategy and where to invest in acquisition
- How to shape acquisition and retention strategy — informs media, channel and proposition choices, though not exact media plans or targeting files
- How to identify true competitors and share threats — informs competitive strategy and brand positioning
- Which partnerships, sponsorships, co-locations or affiliates to pursue — informs partnership strategy, though not commercial viability or deal structure
- How to think about product, range or occasion expansion — informs proposition refinement, though not full category strategy or operational feasibility
- Where to focus geographically — informs where the brand should prioritise effort, though not site economics or full network strategy

### How the data answers these questions

| Question | Data and capability used | How a client lead would walk through it |
|---|---|---|
| Which customer segments matter most? | Demographics metrics (target customer proportion, indexed vs base) split by Affluence, Age, Gender and Lifestage dimensions | Walk through the demographics view to show how the cohort is composed, then use the indexed-vs-base values to highlight where the cohort over- or under-indexes against the benchmark population. |
| How should we shape acquisition and retention? | Category penetration and affinity (target customer penetration, affinity vs base, average spend and transactions per customer with benchmark comparators), Online share of spend | Use category penetration and affinity to show what categories matter most to the cohort, layer in spend and transaction depth against the benchmark to show engagement intensity, then bring in online share of spend to inform channel emphasis. |
| Who are our competitors and what else is winning share? | Brand penetration and affinity within selected categories, Category and brand dimension | Pre-select up to four categories for brand affinities, then walk through brand-level penetration and affinity to show which competing brands the cohort engages with and where indirect threats may sit. |
| Which partnerships or adjacencies are most relevant? | Category and brand affinity, Geographic module (proportion of cohort by location, penetration of population by location) | Use category and brand affinity to surface where the cohort over-indexes, then layer in the geographic module to identify where partner reach or co-location opportunities may sit. |

### Common customer questions or objections

**Q: I already know who my customer is — what does this add?**
Profile iQ can validate or challenge that view using behavioural data across the customer base, rather than survey responses or internal sales views.

**Q: How do we action these insights?**
The solution is designed to produce practical outputs such as segment priorities, partnership opportunities and competitor context. For activation, downstream solutions such as Enrich iQ are typically used.

**Q: How do we justify the ROI?**
The value story is tied to better targeting, less wasted spend and clearer growth opportunities.

**Q: Our teams aren't data-savvy — will they be able to use this?**
The materials position the outputs as business-friendly and designed to be embedded with client lead support.

## Delivery options and capabilities

### Delivery options

Profile iQ is typically delivered as a one-off Excel dashboard, often accompanied by a short PDF summary insights pack. It is engaged on an annual or ad hoc profiling basis depending on client need, with an indicative delivery timeline of around four weeks from signed LOE. The role of the insights pack is currently not standardised — see Known gaps below.

### Core capabilities

| Capability area | Capability |
|---|---|
| Demographics | Age, affluence, lifestage and gender distributions for the target cohort, indexed against benchmark |
| Category penetration and affinity | Penetration, affinity, average spend per customer and average transactions per customer for the target cohort across standard industries, with benchmark comparators |
| Brand penetration and affinity | Brand-level penetration and affinity within selected categories, with four categories pre-selected for brand affinities by default |
| Online behaviour | Target customer online share of spend (of discretionary spend) |
| Geographic | Top locations the cohort comes from, and population penetration by location, available across SA2, SA3, SA4, GCCSA, LGA, Postcode and Suburb |
| Cohort and benchmark setup | Configurable target and benchmark definitions including bespoke segments, with effort scaling from simple brand cohorts through to bespoke threshold-based segments |
| State-level analysis | Standard configuration to run metrics nationally or split by state |
| Category framework | Standard Market Monitor iQ taxonomy (30 broad / 107 lower categories), or a custom client-supplied category structure (custom requires additional setup time and Emerging team sign-off pre-LOE) |

## Components

This section captures the components currently documented in the catalogue Excel for Profile iQ. It is the structured technical view of what the solution contains. For specifics on how each metric is calculated, including transaction filters, customer definitions and aggregation rules, please speak directly to the analyst owner.

### Metrics

| Category | Metric | What it measures | Available values |
|---|---|---|---|
| Demographics | Target customer proportion | Proportion of the target cohort in each demographic segment | Across Affluence, Age, Gender, Lifestage |
| Demographics | Indexed vs base (affinity) | Over/under-representation of target cohort vs benchmark population | — |
| Category penetration and affinity | Target customer penetration | Share of target cohort that transacts in each industry category | Across standard industries |
| Category penetration and affinity | Affinity vs base | Over/under-representation of category penetration vs benchmark | — |
| Category penetration and affinity | Avg spend per customer | Weighted average spend by target cohort customers in each industry category during the analysis period | — |
| Category penetration and affinity | Avg transactions per customer | Weighted average number of transactions by target cohort customers in each industry category | — |
| Category penetration and affinity | Avg spend per customer — benchmark | Weighted average spend by benchmark cohort customers in each industry category during the analysis period | — |
| Category penetration and affinity | Avg transactions per customer — benchmark | Weighted average number of transactions by benchmark cohort customers in each industry category | — |
| Brand penetration and affinity | Target customer penetration | Proportion of the target cohort who have transacted with the given brand within the time period | Default of 50 brands per category in the backend |
| Brand penetration and affinity | Affinity vs base | Over/under-representation of brand penetration vs benchmark | — |
| Online behaviour | Online share of spend | Target customer online spend share of discretionary spend | — |
| Geographic output | Proportion of cohort by location | Top N locations by cohort concentration | Any combination of SA2, SA3, SA4, GCCSA, LGA, Postcode, Suburb. Default top 20 in dashboard; more available in backend exports |
| Geographic output | Penetration of population by location | Percentage of residents in a location who are in the cohort | Same geography options as above |

### Dimensions

| Dimension | Available values |
|---|---|
| Customer cohort | The defined group of customers being profiled — a single project can contain multiple cohorts for side-by-side comparison |
| Affluence | — |
| Age | — |
| Gender | — |
| Lifestage | — |
| Standard industries | 30 broad categories and 107 lower categories based on the Market Monitor iQ industry taxonomy |
| Category and brand | Brand-level penetration and affinity within selected categories; four categories pre-selected for brand affinities by default |
| State | ACT, NSW, NT, QLD, SA, TAS, VIC, WA, or AUS for national aggregate |

### Configurable inputs

| Input | What the client provides |
|---|---|
| Target and benchmark definition (required) | Defines who the target cohort is. Options include brand customers, industry, customer attributes (location, demographics), combination, or bespoke definition (filters, thresholds, brand mining). Effort ranges from around 30 minutes for a simple brand cohort to 4+ hours for bespoke segments requiring workshopping and QA. |
| Benchmark cohort (required) | The population the target cohort is compared against. Defaults to all CBA electronically active customers nationally. Custom benchmarks (e.g. all streaming customers) require the same setup effort as a target cohort definition. |
| Analysis period (required) | Any period. Defaults to 12 months to most recent data available; 12 months strongly recommended. Different periods per profile are possible. |
| Geography level (optional) | Geographic granularity for cohort concentration and population penetration. Options: Postal Area (postcode), Suburb/Locality, SA2, SA3, SA4, GCCSA, LGA. One or more can be configured per project. |
| Analysis level (standard) | National aggregate (AUS) or state-level (ACT, NSW, NT, QLD, SA, TAS, VIC, WA). Defaults to AUS. State-level produces approximately 8x more rows. |
| Top N geographies in output (optional) | How many geography areas appear in cohort distribution and population penetration outputs. Default 15; maximum 20 recommended for dashboard view; higher values available in backend exports. Can be set independently for the distribution and penetration outputs. |
| Pre-selected categories for brand affinities (optional) | Client lead selects four categories in advance for efficiency. Can be changed at any point pre or post delivery. |
| Category framework (optional) | Market Monitor iQ standard taxonomy (default), Custom (client-defined), or Both. Custom requires additional delivery time and effort and must be signed off by the Emerging team before the LOE is signed. |
| Custom category table (add-on) | Required when Category framework is Custom or Both. A client-supplied table mapping brand names and industry codes to bespoke category names. Must contain columns: CAT_NAME, BRAND_NAME, INDUSTRY_LEVEL_3_ID, SHOPPING_CENTRE_KEY. |

### Edge cases and exceptions

The components above describe the standard solution as it is delivered today. The following items are exceptions worth being aware of when discussing scope with clients, grouped by type. Items captured in the Excel but not currently delivered are listed separately in the appendix parking lot, not here.

**Add-ons versus standard.** Online share of spend and top geographies output are standard outputs. Geography-based cohort filtering is an optional configuration. A custom category framework, supplied via a custom category table, is the main add-on — it requires Emerging team sign-off pre-LOE and adds delivery time.

**Constraints on what's available.**

- Maximum 20 geographies by default for dashboard display; higher values are available in backend data exports.
- Brand penetration and affinity defaults to a maximum of 50 brands per category in the backend.
- State-level analysis produces approximately 8x more rows and increases compute time and output volume significantly. Recommended where geographic variation within the cohort is a key client question.
- Setting an analysis period shorter than 12 months will not affect demographics proportions but will reduce signal for penetration and spend metrics.

**Compliance and operational.**

- Bespoke cohort and benchmark definitions add QA effort.
- Custom categorisation increases delivery time and effort and needs Emerging team sign-off before the LOE is signed.

## Relationship to other solutions

### Relationship to adjacent solutions

Profile iQ sits within the Consumer Brands family alongside Market Monitor iQ and Enrich iQ. It is positioned upstream of solutions that use customer insights for activation or ongoing monitoring.

### Known overlaps

The clearest adjacencies are with Enrich iQ and Market Monitor iQ. Profile iQ overlaps with Enrich iQ in that both work with customer-level insights, but Profile iQ is profiling and decision-support whereas Enrich iQ converts those insights into customer targeting and activation. Market Monitor iQ supports ongoing tracking of customer segments rather than the deeper one-off profiling Profile iQ is designed for.

The practical distinction:

- Profile iQ answers, "Who are our customers and how should we think about them?"
- Enrich iQ answers, "How do we activate against these customers?"
- Market Monitor iQ answers, "How are these customer segments performing over time?"

## Risks, gaps and dependencies

### Known gaps or ambiguities

Some existing materials still need tighter wording to distinguish between geography as a cohort filter and top geographies as a standard output — these are two different things and the inconsistency creates confusion. Delivery wording also needs standardising around the one-off Excel dashboard, with the PDF summary insights pack noted where relevant. Pricing needs to be described carefully so the entry point and the practical commercial approach by client type are both clear. The biggest unresolved gap is the role of the insights pack — who owns it, what is standard, and whether it is part of the core solution or an additional manual layer. The current dashboard does not reliably tell the story clients need, which means delivery often leans heavily on manual reworking of an insights pack that sits awkwardly between teams: too manual and tailored for scaled delivery, but too data-manipulation-heavy to sit cleanly with customer-facing teams. This creates an unresolved product and operating model decision, not just a delivery inconvenience. Some example case studies are also explicitly not approved for named external use.

A few additional gaps were surfaced while populating this document from the catalogue Excel and the client lead description, and are flagged here for resolution:

- The catalogue Excel marks the Online behaviour metric and Geographic module metrics as Optional, while the client lead description treats them as Standard capabilities. The two sources need to be reconciled.
- The catalogue Excel records two different defaults for the benchmark cohort: the "Target and benchmark definition" row notes "All Australians" (in an incomplete sentence), while the dedicated "Benchmark cohort" row says default is "all CBA electronically active customers nationally." The correct default needs confirming.
- The "Live as of Start of April" flag is blank for the "Target and benchmark definition" client configurable input, while every other Profile iQ row is marked Y. Likely a data-entry oversight rather than a real exclusion, but worth confirming.

### Key risks in how the solution is described or applied

- Over-claiming Profile iQ as an activation solution when it is better framed as profiling and decision-support, conflating it with general market research rather than behavioural transaction-based intelligence, and selling it without enough discipline on cohort definition, benchmark selection and output configuration. 
- Geography in particular may be misunderstood unless the two different uses (cohort filter vs top-geographies output) are clearly separated. The solution experience may be inconsistent if the dashboard is treated as the core deliverable, but the real storytelling value depends on a manual insights pack with no clear owner or standard. 
- Bespoke segments and custom categorisation also add delivery effort and QA requirements, so there is risk in under-scoping complexity.

### Dependencies

- Agreed cohort and benchmark definitions
- Selected time period and geography settings where relevant
- Client input on bespoke definitions or custom brand and category inclusions
- A clearer decision on ownership, standardisation and production of the insights pack if that remains a core part of the client experience
