## Solution overview

### What this solution is

Centre iQ is a retail property insights solution that helps shopping centre owners and operators understand centre performance, customer behaviour, market share, trade area contribution, and competitive position using de-identified banking transaction data. It combines a web-based portal, Excel dashboard outputs, detailed data extracts, and support services to help clients make better leasing, marketing, asset management, development, research, and centre management decisions.

### What problem or need it addresses

Retail property owners often do not have direct access to customer spend data generated in their centres, which makes it harder to understand how a centre is performing, where growth opportunities sit, how customers behave, and where competitive threats are emerging. Centre iQ addresses this by providing visibility of in-centre spend, market share, customer segments, trade area contribution, and change drivers so clients can make more evidence-based decisions.

### Who it is for

Centre iQ is for shopping centre owners and teams that need centre-level performance intelligence, particularly across leasing, marketing, centre management, asset management, development, and research functions. It is positioned for centres seeking to measure and grow market share, understand where customers are coming from, and optimise commercial decisions based on actual in-centre performance. Current material indicates the standard Centre iQ offer is aimed at Sub Regional to Super Regional centres, plus outlet and CBD centres.

### Positioning

Position this as centre performance intelligence rather than general catchment intelligence alone. The core value is that it includes everything in Resident iQ, then adds the in-centre performance lens: market share, contribution to centre, centre drivers, and Event Monitor. Describe it as a decisioning tool for landlords and centre teams who need to track performance, defend share, identify growth opportunities, and measure the impact of leasing and marketing actions.

### What this solution is for

- Tracking centre performance against trade area demand and benchmarks
- Measuring market share and competitive position over time
- Quantifying trade area and customer segment contribution to centre performance
- Understanding the drivers of spend, visits, and customer penetration
- Identifying customer segments, brand preferences, and crowd composition
- Measuring campaign, event, and activation impact
- Supporting retailer and tenant mix conversations with evidence

### What this solution is not for

- Stand-alone trade area demand or catchment sizing where centre performance is not needed (Resident iQ is the more focused offer)
- Smaller centres or simpler precincts where the full Centre iQ depth is not warranted (Neighbourhood iQ is more appropriate)
- Replacing direct retailer negotiation, rent modelling, or tenancy economics
- Acting as a full business case, valuation, or development feasibility tool
- Establishing causal attribution beyond the metrics provided

## Customer needs and decisions supported

### Customer needs addressed

Clients buy Centre iQ when they need both a view of trade area demand and an evidence-based read on how their centre is actually performing within it. The underlying needs are to track market share over time, understand the drivers of spend, visits, and penetration, identify customer segments and brand preferences, quantify contribution from trade area and crowd, measure the impact of events and activations, and monitor competitor developments. It exists because internal centre reporting alone does not answer these questions — they require a normalised, externally benchmarked view of in-centre performance and the surrounding trade area.

### Common questions

The questions clients ask in this space cluster around the following core themes.

**Core question 1 — How is my centre performing?**

- What is our market share and how is it trending?
- Where are we winning, defending, or losing share, by category and destination?
- How do our drivers compare against benchmarks for similar centres and trade areas?
- What is driving change in spend, visits, customer penetration, and transaction size?

**Core question 2 — Who is shopping with my centre, and how does that compare to my catchment?**

- Who are our centre customers by age, affluence, lifestage, and crowd?
- How does the centre customer base compare to the resident base?
- Where are customers coming from across the trade area and beyond it?
- What brand preferences do they have, and how do they shop in centre versus outside?

**Core question 3 — Where is the growth opportunity?**

- Which categories or customer segments offer the best growth?
- Where is resident spend leaking outside the centre or going online?
- Which trade area sub-areas are over- or under-contributing?
- What does opportunity look like at SA1 level when we deep-dive?

**Core question 4 — Did our actions work?**

- Did this event or campaign drive uplift in customers, visits, and spend share?
- What was the time-of-day and day-of-week pattern?
- How did the centre perform against baseline?

### Common customer questions or objections

**Q: Does this measure catchment demand only, or actual centre performance?**
Centre iQ does both. It includes the catchment views found in Resident iQ, then adds in-centre performance — market share, contribution to centre, Centre Drivers, and Event Monitor.

**Q: How is Centre iQ different from Resident iQ?**
Resident iQ focuses on resident spend within a defined trade area — leakage, brand preferences, segment composition, and total opportunity. Centre iQ adds the centre-performance lens: in-centre spend, market share, contribution, and event analysis. If the client only needs trade area demand, Resident iQ is the more focused option.

**Q: Are monthly views available, or only rolling annual views?**
Most Driver and Benchmark cuts are yearly, with selected measures available monthly (notably Relative Driver Market share by Crowd, and Spend Prop — High Level under Leakage). Date and hour-level views sit within the Event monitor add-on.

**Q: What's included in the subscription?**
A web-based portal, an Excel dashboard, detailed Excel data extracts, support hours, and access to Event monitor as an optional add-on. Optional extensions and consulting-style add-ons are also available.

**Q: Is Centre iQ right for smaller or neighbourhood centres?**
Centre iQ is designed for Sub Regional to Super Regional centres, plus outlet and CBD centres. For smaller or simpler precincts, Neighbourhood iQ is a lighter-weight offer.

**Q: Are the outputs sufficient for retailer conversations, campaign ROI, or trade area review work?**
Fact sheets, brand preferences, and segment data support retailer conversations, and Event monitor supports event and campaign measurement. Centre iQ does not on its own establish full causal attribution and still requires commercial interpretation.

### Edge cases and exceptions

The components above describe the standard solution as it is delivered today. The following items are exceptions worth being aware of when discussing scope with clients. Items captured in the Excel but not currently delivered are listed separately in the appendix parking lot.

**Add-ons versus standard.**

- SA1 Metrics is an Optional add-on providing an SA1-level deep-dive view of Centre Driver penetration, spend per customer, visits per customer, and Relative Driver market share.
- Event monitor is an Optional add-on providing date and hour-level views of relative market share and centre spend proportion, plus monthly customer, visit, and visits-per-customer counts.

**Constraints on what's available.**

- Most Driver and Benchmark cuts are yearly. Monthly is available on selected measures, notably Relative Driver Market share by Crowd, and Spend Prop — High Level under Leakage. Date x Hour is restricted to the Event monitor add-on.
- Drivers — By Crowd cuts use Broad Cat rather than Sub Cat for most metrics. SA1 Metrics use Broad Cat only.
- TA Proportions are produced as two outputs per metric: one with OOA included, one with OOA excluded.
- Brand preferences include In Centre / Outside Centre Brand Flag plus Channel Type cuts.

**Compliance and operational.**

- All outputs are subject to minimum sample size thresholds and bank compliance rules; some results may be aggregated or omitted.
- Outputs depend on access to CommBank iQ's de-identified transaction dataset and the agreed weighting methodology.

## Relationship to other solutions

### Relationship to adjacent solutions

Centre iQ is closely related to Resident iQ. Resident iQ focuses on total resident spend within a defined trade area and is used to understand market opportunity, leakage, brand engagement, and segment composition. Centre iQ includes those catchment insights but extends them with centre-level performance intelligence, including in-centre spend, market share, contribution analysis, and Event Monitor.

It also relates to Centre iQ Lite and Neighbourhood iQ as lighter variants of the broader Centre iQ proposition. Centre iQ Lite appears to retain core components but without ongoing updates. Neighbourhood iQ is a simplified offer for smaller centres with reduced functionality and delivery format.

### Known overlaps

The main overlap is with Resident iQ. Both use the same underlying transaction dataset and both can support leasing, marketing, and strategic planning. The practical distinction is that Resident iQ is focused on catchment demand, while Centre iQ adds actual centre performance measurement. That distinction matters commercially, because Centre iQ answers whether the centre is capturing the opportunity, not just whether the opportunity exists.

There is also overlap with extension products such as Retail Spend Insights, Trade Area Review, and bespoke reporting. These are adjacent offers that deepen or extend the core Centre iQ subscription rather than replacing it.

The practical distinction:

- Centre iQ answers, "How is my centre performing, and is it capturing the opportunity in its trade area?"
- Resident iQ answers, "What is the demand sitting in my trade area, and where does it currently go?"

- Blurring Centre iQ with Resident iQ if the centre performance lens is not stated clearly enough.
- Over-positioning the solution as a complete answer to leasing, development, or marketing decisions when it is better framed as a decision support product.
- Confusion across Centre iQ, Centre iQ Lite, and Neighbourhood iQ variants if packaging, update frequency, and included components are not described consistently.

- Defined trade area structures
- Platform and dashboard delivery
- Support hours and customer enablement
