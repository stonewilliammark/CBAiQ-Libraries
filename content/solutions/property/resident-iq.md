## Solution overview

### What this solution is

Resident iQ is a resident-based retail analytics solution delivered through the CommBank iQ portal and a downloadable dashboard. It shows how residents in a defined trade area spend across retail categories, where they shop, what brands they engage with, and how different customer segments behave. ABS Census inputs and benchmarks are included alongside the spend behaviour data to give a fuller picture of the trade area.

### What problem or need it addresses

Property clients, particularly shopping centre operators, need to understand the real behaviour of people living in their catchment rather than relying on intuition or survey-based views. Resident iQ helps them quantify the size of the local opportunity, measure leakage to other areas or online, identify category and brand gaps, and target customer segments more effectively. It contextualises a centre's resident base against benchmarks and local Census profile to inform leasing, marketing and centre strategy decisions.

### Who it is for

Resident iQ is for retail property clients managing one or more centres who need resident-level trade area insight to support centre strategy, leasing, marketing and planning. Within Portfolio iQ, it sits as a selectable resident insights module rather than being the portfolio umbrella itself.

### Positioning

Position this as a self-serve resident trade area intelligence solution. Its value is in showing the size, composition and behaviour of the resident opportunity around a centre or catchment — how much residents spend, where that spend goes, which categories are over- or under-indexed, which brands resonate, and which Crowds segments represent the strongest opportunity. It is not a centre performance tool in the same sense as Centre iQ; the underlying data is based on trade area residents, not centre-level behaviour.

### What this solution is for

- Understanding resident spend by category and sub-category.
- Identifying leakage to online and competing locations.
- Assessing brand engagement and affinity among residents.
- Understanding the trade area through Crowds iQ and Census views.
- Supporting leasing and marketing decisions with resident evidence.

### What this solution is not for

- Measuring centre visits, centre market share or event performance.
- Analysing in-centre customer behaviour at the same level as Centre iQ.
- Treating suburb-level spend as a precise centre measure — suburb can only be a proxy because spend includes retail beyond the centre itself.
- Replacing Portfolio iQ, which is the umbrella subscription construct covering multiple centres and modules. Resident iQ is one of the selectable modules within that broader structure.

## Customer needs and decisions supported

### Customer needs addressed

Clients in this space are trying to quantify the retail market opportunity within a defined trade area, understand where local residents are spending outside the trade area or online, identify category and brand gaps that could improve leasing outcomes, understand key consumer segments for better-targeted marketing, and benchmark local resident behaviour against broader geographies.

### Common questions

The questions clients ask in this space cluster around the following core themes.

**Core question 1 — Which categories represent the strongest local opportunity?**

- Where are residents spending most by category and sub-category?
- Which categories are over- or under-indexed against benchmarks?
- How does the opportunity differ across trade area types?

**Core question 2 — Where is leakage occurring, and what is contestable?**

- How much resident spend is captured locally versus online or in other locations?
- Which suburbs are taking the most spend from my catchment?
- Where is contestable spend concentrated by category or by Crowd?

**Core question 3 — Which brands or retailers are most relevant for leasing conversations?**

- Which brands have the strongest penetration among residents?
- Which brands over-index on affinity?
- How does brand engagement differ by category and Crowd?

**Core question 4 — Which customer segments should be prioritised?**

- Which Crowds segments dominate the trade area?
- How do segment behaviours differ by category, spend and leakage?
- Which segments offer the strongest growth opportunity?

### Supporting decisions

| Decision | How this solution helps | What it does not answer on its own |
|---|---|---|
| What is the size and shape of the local retail opportunity? | Resident drivers, expenditure metrics and benchmark comparisons at trade area type and category level. | It does not assess whether the centre has the physical or operational capacity to capture the opportunity. |
| Where is leakage occurring and what is contestable? | Destination of spend by suburb and online, available at trade area and sub-category level, with Crowd cuts. | It does not isolate exact centre share or fully explain leakage drivers. |
| Which categories or brands should we prioritise for leasing? | Brand penetration and brand affinity by trade area, resident/shopper, and category, with Crowd cuts. | It does not replace tenant feasibility, commercial terms or leasing judgement. |
| Which customer segments should we focus on for marketing? | Top Crowds, drivers by Crowd, destination of spend by Crowd, brand preferences by Crowd, plus Crowd population. | It does not determine the best channel, campaign or execution plan. |

### How the data answers these questions

| Question | Data and capability used | How a client lead would walk through it |
|---|---|---|
| Which categories represent the strongest local opportunity? | Resident drivers (spend per capita, penetration, transactions per customer, transaction size) by trade area type and sub-category, with benchmark comparison. | Start at the broad category view of resident drivers across the trade area, compare to benchmarks to spot over- or under-indexed categories, then drill into sub-categories to identify where the opportunity is most concentrated. |
| Where is leakage occurring, and what is contestable? | Destination of spend (Spend Prop — Detailed) by suburb and online, available at trade area and sub-category level, including by Crowd. | Open the destination of spend view, look at the suburb and online split for the trade area, then narrow to the categories or Crowds where leakage is largest. Frame in-trade-area spend as contestable while highlighting online and competitor locations. |
| Which brands or retailers are most relevant for leasing conversations? | Brand penetration and brand affinity by trade area, resident/shopper, and sub-category, with Crowd cuts available. | Use brand preferences to identify brands residents already engage with, compare penetration and affinity against benchmark to see where engagement is stronger or weaker, and use Crowd cuts to refine for the segments most relevant to the centre. |
| Which customer segments should be prioritised? | Crowds iQ views — top Crowds, drivers by Crowd, destination of spend by Crowd, brand preferences by Crowd, and Crowd population. | Start with top Crowds in the trade area, then unpack each one through drivers, destination and brand preferences to assess size, spend, leakage and brand behaviour. Use this to rank which segments warrant marketing focus. |

### Common customer questions or objections

**Q: Can the trade area be customised?**
Yes. Trade areas can use standard TTA, MTA or PTA structures, or be refined with the team to fit the centre's catchment. Trade areas have been delivered at SA1-level building blocks where greater precision is needed.

**Q: Can benchmarks be customised?**
Yes. Benchmarks can be national, by centre type, or against a defined peer set, depending on what comparison is most useful for the conversation.

**Q: Will all requested outputs always be available?**
No. Reporting is subject to minimum sample size thresholds and bank compliance rules, and some cuts may be aggregated or suppressed where thresholds are not met.

## Delivery options and capabilities

### Delivery options

Resident iQ is delivered through web-based access in the CommBank iQ / Portfolio iQ client portal and a downloadable dashboard file. Self-serve dashboards include benchmarks and Census outputs as part of the resident module. Updates are typically quarterly, with data generally expected around three weeks after the end of the relevant period. History depth covers 12-month, year-on-year and monthly 24-month views for relevant metrics.

### Core capabilities

| Capability area | Capability |
|---|---|
| Market overview | Resident expenditure, population, spend per capita, in-trade-area, out-of-area and online mix. |
| Resident drivers | Spend per capita, penetration, transaction frequency and transaction size at trade area and category level. |
| Monthly views | Monthly trend views for resident drivers and destination of spend. |
| Destination of spend | Suburb and online distribution, including top spend locations. |
| Brand preferences | Penetration and affinity by category and trade area. |
| Crowds iQ views | Top Crowds, spend drivers by Crowd, destination of spend by Crowd, brand preferences by Crowd. |
| Resident profile (Census) | Household, income, birthplace, language, religion and related area profile metrics. |
| Benchmarks | Comparison to national or selected geographies (25th and 75th percentile bands). |
| Category structure | All Retail plus broad categories such as Apparel, Department Stores, Food Catering, Food Retail and Liquor, General Retail, Household Goods, Jewellery Watch and Luxury, Leisure, Retail Services, Supermarkets and Entertainment. |

## Relationship to other solutions

### Relationship to adjacent solutions

Resident iQ sits alongside Centre iQ as a separate module within the broader Portfolio iQ structure. Portfolio iQ is the umbrella subscription construct for clients with multiple centres, while Resident iQ is one of the selectable insight modules. Centre iQ focuses on centre-level views and outputs; Resident iQ focuses on trade area resident behaviour.

### Known overlaps

There is overlap with Centre iQ in areas such as categories, benchmarks, brand preferences, Crowds and trade area framing.

The practical distinction:

- Resident iQ answers, "How do residents in my trade area spend, where does that spend go, and which segments matter?"
- Centre iQ answers, "How is my centre performing, who is visiting, and how are they engaging with the centre?"

## Risks, gaps and dependencies

### Known gaps or ambiguities

The source set blends Resident iQ with Portfolio iQ material, and the user guide still uses the label "Centre iQ — Resident View", so naming and packaging can create confusion if not handled carefully. The cleanest definition is that Resident iQ is the resident insights module or offer, not the umbrella portfolio construct.

### Key risks in how the solution is described or applied

The main risk is blending Resident iQ with Portfolio iQ or Centre iQ in client conversations, which can mis-set expectations on what the solution measures. A second risk is overstating what resident destination metrics mean for centre performance — suburb-level spend should only be treated as a proxy for centre share, and outputs still require interpretation and local knowledge.

### Dependencies

- A defined trade area, at SA1 level in the engagement example.
- Access through the CommBank iQ / Portfolio iQ portal.
- De-identified CBA transaction data, normalised to population behaviour.
- ABS Census inputs and benchmark geographies.
- Minimum sample and commercial aggregation thresholds, which may suppress or aggregate some outputs.
