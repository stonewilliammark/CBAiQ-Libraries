## Solution overview

### What this solution is

Retail Spend Insights is a modelled view of resident spend per capita across Australia, delivered at SA1 level so clients can aggregate it into bespoke catchments. It provides a consistent base view of local market size by expenditure and supports analysis across broad retail, fine retail and adjacent consumption categories. The solution includes online and in-store views where available, alongside supporting benchmark and methodology material to help clients interpret the data correctly.

### What problem or need it addresses

Retail Spend Insights addresses a common retail property problem: owners and advisers often lack a robust, consistent view of how much residents in a local area typically spend. Without that, teams rely on proxies, broad assumptions, inconsistent catchments or outdated market sources, which weakens feasibility work, category opportunity assessment and investment cases. The solution provides a reusable, sector-accepted base view that helps clients size local market opportunity more credibly and defend the assumptions underneath property decisions.

### Who it is for

Retail Spend Insights is primarily for retail property consultants advising shopping centre owners. It is best suited to teams that need a reusable spend input for feasibility, leasing, development, strategy and investment work, and that are capable of constructing catchments and interpreting the output in a commercial property context. It can also suit consultants with narrower category, geography or project-specific needs where a reduced-scope delivery is more appropriate than a national subscription.

### Positioning

Position this as a foundational market-sizing input, not a finished answer in its own right. The value lies in giving consultants a sector-recognised, consistent view of resident spend that can be flexed into bespoke catchments and used repeatedly across assets and engagements. Frame it as a way to build a more accurate view of local market size and category demand, rather than as a substitute for property expertise, centre performance data or customer analytics.

### What this solution is for

- Understanding average resident spend in an area by category
- Building bespoke catchments from SA1-level building blocks
- Supporting local market sizing, feasibility, leasing and investment work
- Creating a consistent base view across multiple assets, projects or client engagements

### What this solution is not for

- Actual centre sales or shopper behaviour
- Market share, leakage or customer segmentation questions
- Direct resale or redistribution of the underlying insights
- Exposing full SA1-level data to end clients in original form
- Replacing commercial property judgement or advisory interpretation

## Customer needs and decisions supported

### Customer needs addressed

Retail property consultants need a consistent and accepted local spend view that supports more defensible market sizing assumptions. They benefit from more granular category visibility than broad market averages provide, and from a reusable dataset that can support multiple projects over time. The solution gives them a practical way to compare local demand against a benchmark rather than relying on guesswork or stitched-together sources.

### Common questions

The questions clients ask in this space cluster around the following core themes.

**Core question 1 — How large is the local market opportunity for a shopping centre, precinct or development?**

**Core question 2 — Which categories appear deeper or thinner in a given market?**

**Core question 3 — How should a consultant defend the market view in a development, acquisition or strategy paper?**

### Common customer questions or objections

**Q: Can we get proxy data elsewhere?**
Proxy data is available, but Retail Spend Insights is valuable not just because it is data — it provides a consistent, sector-recognised base view that is easier to defend in real property decisions.

**Q: Do we have to take the full national set? It is too expensive.**
Where a full national licence is too broad, the solution can be framed as a scoped subset or project delivery covering reduced categories, reduced geographic granularity, or limited state coverage.

**Q: Does this answer market share, leakage or segmentation?**
No. Where the client is actually asking market share, leakage or segmentation questions, another solution or broader package should be introduced rather than stretching Retail Spend Insights beyond its role.

## Relationship to other solutions

### Relationship to adjacent solutions

Retail Spend Insights sits as a foundational market-sizing input within a broader retail property solution set. It is often the right starting point when a client needs to understand the size and shape of local demand. Where the client then needs to know who the customers are, how a centre is performing, whether spend is leaking, or how brands and competitors are performing, the conversation should move into adjacent solutions rather than overextending Retail Spend Insights. Source material indicates alignment to Centre iQ category mapping where possible, while keeping Retail Spend Insights distinct as a separate reusable spend input.

### Known overlaps

The main overlap is with property consulting, feasibility and strategy work. The practical distinction is that Retail Spend Insights provides the base market view, while the consultant supplies the catchment logic, contextual interpretation and commercial recommendation. There may also be superficial overlap with broader CommBank iQ analytics offers, but this solution should not be positioned as a substitute for solutions focused on customer, brand, competition, market share or leakage questions.

## Risks, gaps and dependencies

### Known gaps or ambiguities

There is also a draft history in some collateral around partner and packaging variants, which should not be over-emphasised unless they remain current.

### Key risks in how the solution is described or applied

- Describing the solution too broadly. It should not be sold as centre sales, shopper behaviour, market share, leakage, segmentation or a finished recommendation.
- Allowing clients to use the underlying data in ways that amount to redistribution, direct on-sale or substitute products. The safest and strongest positioning is as a licensed, structured input used within broader advisory and decision-making workflows.

### Dependencies

- Category mapping, outlier treatment and aggregation rules
- Benchmark, methodology and caveat documentation
- Client capability to construct catchments and interpret results
- Licence controls and use-case restrictions governing downstream use
