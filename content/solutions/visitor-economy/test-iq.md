## Solution overview

### What this solution is

Test iQ is a visitor economy insights solution that helps destination management organisations, tourism bodies, accommodation operators, and local government authorities understand destination performance, visitor behaviour, origin market composition, and competitive position using de-identified banking transaction data. It combines a web-based portal, Excel dashboard outputs, detailed data extracts, and support services to help clients make better destination planning, marketing, product development, event programming, and investment decisions.

### What problem or need it addresses

Destination and tourism operators often lack a clear, real-time view of visitor spend generated within their destination, which makes it difficult to understand performance trends, identify high-value visitor segments, track seasonal variation, and assess competitive position relative to other destinations. Test iQ addresses this by providing visibility of in-destination spend, origin market composition, visitor segment behaviour, and seasonal demand patterns so clients can make more evidence-based decisions about where to invest, how to attract visitors, and what experiences to grow.

### Who it is for

Test iQ is for destination management organisations, state and local tourism bodies, regional development agencies, and accommodation or experience operators who need destination-level visitor intelligence across marketing, product development, events, planning, and investment functions. It is positioned for destinations seeking to measure and grow visitor spend, understand where visitors are coming from, track seasonal and event-driven demand, and optimise commercial decisions based on actual visitor performance. Current material indicates the standard Test iQ offer is aimed at regional and major urban destinations, as well as defined tourism precincts and event corridors.

### Positioning

Position this as destination performance intelligence rather than general visitor demand sizing alone. The core value is that it includes the origin market and demand layer from Region iQ, then adds the in-destination performance lens: visitor spend share, origin market contribution, destination drivers, and Season Monitor. Describe it as a decisioning tool for destination managers and tourism teams who need to track performance, defend spend share, identify high-value origin markets, and measure the impact of events and campaigns.

### What this solution is for

- Tracking destination performance against origin market demand and benchmarks
- Measuring visitor spend share and competitive position over time
- Quantifying origin market and visitor segment contribution to destination performance
- Understanding the drivers of spend, visits, and visitor penetration
- Identifying visitor segments, activity preferences, and experience composition
- Measuring campaign, event, and season impact on visitor spend
- Supporting operator and investment conversations with evidence

### What this solution is not for

- Stand-alone origin market demand sizing where destination performance is not needed (Region iQ is the more focused offer)
- Smaller or simpler visitor precincts where the full Test iQ depth is not warranted (Precinct iQ is more appropriate)
- Replacing direct operator negotiation, yield management, or accommodation feasibility
- Acting as a full business case, grant assessment, or infrastructure feasibility tool
- Establishing causal attribution beyond the metrics provided

## Customer needs and decisions supported

### Customer needs addressed

Clients buy Test iQ when they need both a view of origin market demand and an evidence-based read on how their destination is actually performing within it. The underlying needs are to track visitor spend share over time, understand the drivers of spend, visits, and visitor penetration, identify high-value segments and experience preferences, quantify contribution from origin markets and visitor types, measure the impact of events and campaigns, and monitor competing destinations. It exists because internal visitor arrival data alone does not answer these questions — they require a normalised, externally benchmarked view of in-destination performance and the surrounding origin market landscape.

### Common questions

The questions clients ask in this space cluster around the following core themes.

**Core question 1 — How is my destination performing?**

- What is our visitor spend share and how is it trending?
- Where are we winning, defending, or losing share, by category and experience type?
- How do our drivers compare against benchmarks for similar destinations?
- What is driving change in visitor spend, trip frequency, penetration, and average spend per visit?

**Core question 2 — Who is visiting my destination, and how does that compare to my origin markets?**

- Who are our destination visitors by origin market, purpose of travel, lifestage, and visitor type?
- How does the in-destination visitor mix compare to the broader origin market potential?
- Where are visitors coming from across domestic and international origin markets?
- What experience preferences do they have, and how do they spend in-destination versus at competing destinations?

**Core question 3 — Where is the growth opportunity?**

- Which experience categories or visitor segments offer the best growth?
- Where is origin market spend leaking to competing destinations or not converting to overnight stays?
- Which origin markets or corridors are over- or under-contributing?
- What does opportunity look like at SA2 level when we deep-dive into a particular corridor?

**Core question 4 — Did our actions work?**

- Did this event, campaign, or activation drive uplift in visitors, trips, and spend share?
- What was the time-of-day and day-of-week pattern during the event window?
- How did the destination perform against baseline in the pre- and post-period?

### Common customer questions or objections

**Q: Does this measure origin market demand only, or actual destination performance?**
Test iQ does both. It includes the origin market views found in Region iQ, then adds in-destination performance — visitor spend share, contribution to destination, Destination Drivers, and Season Monitor.

**Q: How is Test iQ different from Region iQ?**
Region iQ focuses on visitor spend potential within a defined origin market — leakage, experience preferences, segment composition, and total opportunity. Test iQ adds the destination-performance lens: in-destination spend, visitor spend share, contribution, and seasonal analysis. If the client only needs origin market demand sizing, Region iQ is the more focused option.

**Q: Are monthly views available, or only rolling annual views?**
Most Driver and Benchmark cuts are yearly, with selected measures available monthly (notably Relative Driver Visitor Spend Share by Visitor Type, and Spend Prop — High Level under Leakage). Date and hour-level views sit within the Season Monitor add-on.

**Q: What's included in the subscription?**
A web-based portal, an Excel dashboard, detailed Excel data extracts, support hours, and access to Season Monitor as an optional add-on. Optional extensions and consulting-style add-ons are also available.

**Q: Is Test iQ right for smaller or niche visitor precincts?**
Test iQ is designed for regional and major urban destinations, as well as defined event corridors and gateway destinations. For smaller or simpler precincts, Precinct iQ is a lighter-weight offer.

**Q: Are the outputs sufficient for operator conversations, campaign ROI, or origin market review work?**
Destination profile outputs, experience preferences, and segment data support operator conversations, and Season Monitor supports event and campaign measurement. Test iQ does not on its own establish full causal attribution and still requires commercial interpretation.

### Edge cases and exceptions

**Add-ons versus standard.**

- SA2 Metrics is an Optional add-on providing an SA2-level deep-dive view of Destination Driver penetration, spend per visitor, trips per visitor, and Relative Driver visitor spend share.
- Season Monitor is an Optional add-on providing date and hour-level views of relative visitor spend share and destination spend proportion, plus monthly visitor, trip, and trips-per-visitor counts.

**Constraints on what's available.**

- Most Driver and Benchmark cuts are yearly. Monthly is available on selected measures, notably Relative Driver Visitor Spend Share by Visitor Type, and Spend Prop — High Level under Leakage. Date x Hour is restricted to the Season Monitor add-on.
- Drivers — By Visitor Type cuts use Broad Cat rather than Sub Cat for most metrics. SA2 Metrics use Broad Cat only.
- Origin Proportions are produced as two outputs per metric: one with OOA included, one with OOA excluded.
- Experience preferences include In Destination / Outside Destination Experience Flag plus Channel Type cuts.

**Compliance and operational.**

- All outputs are subject to minimum sample size thresholds and bank compliance rules; some results may be aggregated or omitted.
- Outputs depend on access to CommBank iQ's de-identified transaction dataset and the agreed weighting methodology.

## Relationship to other solutions

### Relationship to adjacent solutions

Test iQ is closely related to Region iQ. Region iQ focuses on total visitor spend potential within a defined origin market and is used to understand market opportunity, leakage, experience engagement, and segment composition. Test iQ includes those origin market insights but extends them with destination-level performance intelligence, including in-destination spend, visitor spend share, contribution analysis, and Season Monitor.

It also relates to Test iQ Lite and Precinct iQ as lighter variants of the broader Test iQ proposition. Test iQ Lite appears to retain core components but without ongoing updates. Precinct iQ is a simplified offer for smaller tourism precincts with reduced functionality and delivery format.

### Known overlaps

The main overlap is with Region iQ. Both use the same underlying transaction dataset and both can support product development, marketing, and strategic planning. The practical distinction is that Region iQ is focused on origin market demand, while Test iQ adds actual destination performance measurement.

- Test iQ answers, "How is my destination performing, and is it capturing the opportunity from its origin markets?"
- Region iQ answers, "What is the visitor spend potential sitting in my origin markets, and where does it currently go?"

## Risks, gaps and dependencies

### Known gaps or ambiguities

Some pricing material is marked draft, and the FY26 pricing slide notes that some origin market thresholds may need updating. Packaging and wording across Test iQ, Test iQ Lite, and Precinct iQ is not yet fully standardised, which can make the distinction between variants harder to communicate.

### Key risks in how the solution is described or applied

- Blurring Test iQ with Region iQ if the destination performance lens is not stated clearly enough.
- Over-positioning the solution as a complete answer to product development, infrastructure, or marketing decisions when it is better framed as a decision support product.
- Confusion across Test iQ, Test iQ Lite, and Precinct iQ variants if packaging, update frequency, and included components are not described consistently.

### Dependencies

- Access to CommBank iQ's de-identified transaction dataset and weighting methodology
- Defined origin market structures
- Platform and dashboard delivery
- Support hours and customer enablement
- Sample size thresholds and bank compliance rules that may suppress some outputs where data is insufficient
